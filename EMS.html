Medical Triaging System

Background

Field paramedics (EMS) are responsible for reporting patient information during ambulance transfer to the receiving trauma center (ER). Usually the dispatch coordinator (PDC) handles these calls, which include information on patient vitals, past medical history, the mechanism of injury, and other on-scene factors.

User Research

We observed the practices within the EMS team and ER trauma team to understand their daily routine and issues associated with triage communication. We developed a user flowchart to highlight each user’s main tasks and an experience map to show their interactions and pains.

Challenges

The main challenge was prioritizing the issues when there was so much interconnectivity between all the users. From multiple interviews, there was inconsistency between the EMT reporting and the PDC reporting due to way every person reports. This adds to the difficulty of prioritizing the issues.

Due to the complexity of the healthcare system, we chose to focus on call prioritization and consistent reporting of patient information. These, in turn, will decrease the over/undertriage rates and the ER response time.
