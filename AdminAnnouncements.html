Homepage Announcements
Admin CMS App
Goal

Designed a solution for admins to create, edit, and manage announcements that appear on the home page.

Client

Cisco Systems

Achievements

  • Scalable: Can handle the creation of numerous announcements with the ability to edit and archive.
  • Flexible: Can handle multiple types of content (text, photo, link) and display on any page on the site.
  • Powerful: Gives admins the ability to manage their own announcements without requesting the development team to add manually into the database.

The Problem

Cisco's collaboration learning platform needed a serious facelift for the homepage. The service team wanted to be able to manage news and other learning tools on the homepage for users to see, but the current process required the development team to manually do this through the database.

This would take time away from the product team to manage verbiage, photos, and other content. Because of this, the former homepage (shown below) was very simple and above the fold, and did not have any indication of the website's purpose.

This two-sided problem turned into an opportunity to improve the experience for both the administrators and the users, so that admins can promote important news through the homepage, and the users can learn more about this collaboration platform.

Design Goals

I first scoped out the problem to understand the effort, time, and needs from the service team. We met on a daily basis to discuss the requirements for the first iteration of the homepage and admin platform and narrowed them down to the following:

  • Admins are tasked with many responsibilities, and we wanted to give them a simple and quick method to handle just one of their daily activities so they can be as productive as possible.
  • The new interface should eliminate the time spent discussing back and forth between the service and the development team to add a new announcement. The service team should be able to just do this themselves.
  • The app should have a full record on all announcements created in the platform.
  • The homepage should include important news and information about collaboration, and be easy to navigate.

Early Homepage Explorations

I designed several iterations of the homepage announcements that would be located near the top of the screen.

The design below was great for scanning all the news at once, but it felt too cluttered when placed in the layout of the page. There was too much text and it broke the flow of the page.

The following design was great for pushing the news as part of the 'Welcome' text, but it was not practical for any more than 3 announcements. We considered arrows or navigation dots to indicate multiple sets of announcements, but it became a technical limitation when we discussed responsiveness.

Final Homepage

The final homepage design was one that was responsive, scannable and worked with the flow of the page. It featured a carousel to house multiple announcements, and it was visible above-the-fold to help users smoothly transition down the page.

Admin Functionality

The next challenge was to design a CMS capable of handling numerous announcements. I iterated through the flow of the admin app (shown below), keeping the visual style consistent with the Cisco branding guidelines. After mapping out the screens, I built a quick prototype to test with the admins.

The admin app had to be very simple but powerful enough to handle any type of announcement on any page. The main page is divided by a list of published, drafts, and archived announcements. The admins have full capability to create, edit, publish, and delete announcements with this app, essentially eliminating the time spent requesting the development team to do this.

Conclusion

After multiple iterations of the homepage and the admin platform, the final solution turned out to be much simpler and more powerful than initially anticipated. The service team can now manage their own announcements quickly, and users can now learn more about the new offerings from the platform.
