Glide Slide
Transfer Bench Product Design

Goal

The Glide Slide was developed as a transfer bench to better assist paraplegics into and out of the shower.

Client

Dr. Cassie Mitchell, Professor at Georgia Tech

Team

Stephanie Zhang
Amy Zhen
Jimmy Zhou

Problem

The transfer bench is a medical device that allows orthopedics to safely transfer into and out of the bathtub, but fails to consider the needs of other users, such as paraplegics. Paraplegia is the condition characterized by immobility and lack of sensation in the lower body, which makes activities of daily living (ADLs) difficult and even dangerous.

Our team developed a solution to provide a safe, comfortable showering experience for young and active paraplegics without the assistance of caregivers.

Market Research

We tested the most common transfer bench in the market and listed the following pros and cons:
    Pros
  • Lightweight
  • Adjustable height
  • Easily cleaned
  • Able to disassemble
    Cons
  • Unstable
  • Slippery material
  • Difficult to assemble

User Research

Dr. Cassie Mitchell, a Georgia Tech professor and Paralympic discus thrower, mentioned that slipping and falling was her biggest fear when using the shower. We converted her needs into design specifications and prioritized them into a pairwise comparison chart.

Ideation

The first design concepts ranged from minimal alteration to the handlebars of the bathtub to a complete redesign of the user environment. These concepts provided a wide scope of possibilities, which allowed us to converge our thinking to simpler designs.

With the insight gained from interviews with Dr. Mitchell and other paraplegics, we developed three main concept models:

  • Retroversion (ergonomic curvature of the seat)
  • Portable version (fan)
  • Complete bathtub redesign

After discussing with various users, we discovered that the retroversion on a stationary device (for home use) would provide a safe and comfortable method of transfer, and was reasonable in terms of cost and efficiency.

Prototype

We modeled the seat panels from wood ribs and stringers to strengthen the prototype for testing. The wood was lasercut, while the aluminum frames were modified from the original device. Two clamps were attached to both legs on one end to mimic the clamping mechanism.

Solution

Glide Slide solves the current issues by:

  • Expanding the backrest to the length of the bench to prevent falling or imbalance
  • Seat curvature based on anthropometric data to prevent slipping
  • Handles in seat for better stabilization when showering
  • Quick release height adjustment
  • Quick release clamps to bathtub edge

These changes allowed universality and stability of the device, making the Glide Slide adjustable to any type of bathtub environment without compromising form and durability.
