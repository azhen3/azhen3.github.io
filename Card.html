Christmas Card Project
Illustration

Summary

I designed and crafted handmade Christmas cards for my friends and family. Several of them were lasercut from cardstock and personalized with a handwritten letter. These are side projects I enjoy doing in my free time.

Inspiration

I decided to create my own cards that was unique in its design and production. To show my passion in technology and design, I thought making a lasercut card was the perfect solution. I enjoy using rapid prototyping tools, and I felt that incorporating the laser cutter into my design process would further expand my knowledge.

For this set of designs, I wanted to practice my typography and layout skills. I took common greetings like “Merry Christmas” and turned them into active illustrations that were incorporated with the shapes. I created my own typography that transformed to the ornament and stocking shape.

Before lasercutting, I made sure there were no extra lines that might create unintentional cuts. This helped me learn more about the preparation required when considering how the final product would be produced.

For this set of designs, I wished to explore color and illustration. For “Holly Jolly Christmas,” I sketched details in type, creating an elegant and unique form that was consistent with the entire piece.

In the following designs, I used color to animate the pieces, creating fun and quirky graphics to illustrate the joyfulness of the holidays.
