Cisco Admin Announcements
CMS for News
Goal

Designed a solution for admins of MyCollab to create, edit, and manage announcements on the home page.

Client

Cisco Systems

Achievements

  • Scalable: Can handle the creation of numerous announcements with the ability to edit and archive.
  • Flexible: Can handle multiple types of content (text, photo, link) and display on any page on the site.
  • Powerful: Gives admins the ability to manage their own announcements without requesting the development team to add manually into the database.

The Problem

Introduce role at Cisco, and MyCollab. (2 sentences)

Admins would always contact the Dev team if they needed to add or update announcements on the home page. This would take time away from the dev team to input correct verbiage, photo, description, link, whereas it would be much simpler to give admins permission to edit content.

Our Design Goals

Short blurb on how you came up with the design goals with the stakeholders

  • Admins are tasked with many responsibilities, and we wanted to give them a simple and quick method to handle just one of their daily activities so they can be as productive as possible.
  • The new interface should eliminate the time spent discussing back and forth between the service and the development team to add a new announcement. The service team should be able to just do this themselves.
  • The app should have a full record on all announcements created in MyCollab.
  • The homepage should include important news and information about collaboration, and be easy to navigate.

User-facing Homepage

Show iterations of the homepage, and describe the challenges involved with each one. Do a side by side comparison.

Challenges

Describe the challenges involved with the first iteration = it got too complex.

V1 was organized by groups and users, where you can see who belonged to a certain group and vice versa for the user view. When it was tested with the service team, we realized very quickly that the user view was not as useful as the group view. The workflow started with a specific admin app where a person could give someone permission. It usually works the other way around, so we decided to take out the complexity and only show a group view.

Solution

Show your final iteration and images

Final iteration is simple, with only the main page listing the groups and the group page listing the people with access. This makes it quick and simple for any user to view the lists and also add/remove permissions.
