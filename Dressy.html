Problem

The initial problem actually originated from Georgia Tech’s student-led Facebook thrift shop group, where students could buy and sell goods ranging from academic supplies to apartment leases.

We saw that many of them were content enough with using the Facebook group if they were buying/selling items of one-time use, mostly being textbooks and furniture that is only needed temporarily. Several interviewees mentioned their struggle to find formalwear, especially sorority students having a hard time finding the right dress. We saw this pain point as an opportunity to develop a faster, more convenient, and cost-effective transaction system for sorority members.

User Research

Based on these interviews, we realized that sorority members spend a significant amount of time searching for a dress per each formal event. They want to wear something different, but don’t want to be seen with someone with the same dress. Each girl wants to feel important, and the dress is a huge factor in that.

On the other hand, girls who own dresses only wear them of maximum twice per year. This means the dress is just collecting dust in the closet. Other sorority members may borrow the dress, but there is no formal transaction associated with the rental. Most of the time the borrowers take forever to return the dress, which is inconvenient for the lender. Oftentimes the lender would be friends with the borrower, but felt weird asking them for money to borrow the dress. This greatly limits their options only lending dresses to close friends.

Solution

We developed a user-friendly mobile app that streamlines the transaction process to make finding the perfect dress much easier. We wanted to create an interface that our audience is already familiar with, pulling inspiration from Pinterest, Twitter, and Facebook. Below show selected screens.
Users can create a profile personalized to size, style, and location. This Pinterest-style news feed makes it easy for users to filter and find great dresses around their area.
Profile allows users to post dresses, view followers and favorites, and edit their list of uploaded posts.
Users can post a dress quickly by taking a photo or uploading and filling out the description. Users can also set to Public or Friends Only, and can allow renters to try on before renting.
Selecting a dress will open a popup with more description and options to like, try on, and rent the dress.
Users can view their cart and edit items, such as payment method (which can be integrated with Paypal or Venmo) and rental period.
