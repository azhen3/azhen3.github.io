Habitat
Zero-Waste Living

Summary

Habitat helps motivate individuals seeking to live a zero-waste lifestyle through gamification.

Making It Stick

Developing good habits is hard. Since moving to California, I have been more observant of how we treat our environment. Before, in my hometown, you would probably see cashiers double-bag your groceries (for heavy items). Here in California, I would be charged a bag fee. This difference in attitude was drastic enough for me to be concerned about how we really live with so much waste. Thus, I tried completing the 30 day zero-waste challenge, but failed miserably.

Many times we are too extreme with changing our habits. I saw this as an opportunity to help motivate individuals who want to live with less waste, but one step at a time through a gamified experience.

Who Will Benefit

I focused on two types of people who could benefit from this app. One type of user, which I would fall under, wishes to develop good habits on living sustainably, but doesnâ€™t necessarily know where to begin. The other type of user can be kids, where this application can teach and motivate them to learn how to live with less waste.

Grow Your Garden

Because this was geared towards sustainable living, I thought the theme of gardening made a lot of sense. I brainstormed ways to simulate growing a garden, ideating on shopping for plants, completing habits associated with each plant that keep your garden healthy, and validating the ease of entry.

Challenges

The more ideas I generated, the more complex it seemed to get. I started focusing too much on the details of the game logistics and less on the important actions of tracking your habits. Bringing gamification to help you reach your lifestyle goals should not be as complex as applications that are merely serve as games.

Simplicity was the key factor to developing this experience, as it should be easy for any user to complete habits without the game element to complicate the app. I decided to strip out extraneous details, such as adding gardening tools as health points on the garden, or having multiple ways to keep your garden healthy.
